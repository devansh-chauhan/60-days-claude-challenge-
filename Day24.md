Day 24 – Startup Business Viability & Investment Analysis
Overview
For Day 24 of the 60 Days of Claude Challenge, I expanded the startup validation work from previous days by evaluating whether the proposed Placement & Interview Preparation Platform for Engineering Students can become a sustainable business.

The analysis focused on business fundamentals rather than product features, including revenue potential, go-to-market strategy, customer acquisition, competitive positioning, and investment readiness.

Final Verdict
🟡 VALIDATE

The opportunity shows strong demand signals and a large addressable market, but customer willingness to pay, acquisition costs, and distribution effectiveness require real-world validation before scaling.

Key Learnings
Distribution often matters more than features.

Revenue is more important than vanity metrics.

Customer interviews are critical before building.

A focused MVP can reduce risk and accelerate validation.

Sustainable businesses are built on repeatable acquisition and retention, not just product quality.

Outcome
Created a comprehensive startup business strategy report and visual dashboard analyzing the feasibility of building a scalable EdTech startup targeting engineering students preparing for placements and interviews.
