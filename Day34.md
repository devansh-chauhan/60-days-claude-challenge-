#  Marketing Detective

**Day 34 – 60 Days of Claude Challenge**

An interactive detective-style learning game that teaches marketing analysis through fictional case investigations. Instead of simply reading marketing concepts, users investigate campaign failures, organize evidence, identify the root cause, and learn how experienced marketers think.

---

##  Project Overview

Marketing Detective transforms marketing strategy into a detective investigation.

Players receive randomized marketing case files and analyze campaign performance by reviewing evidence, identifying clues, and solving the primary marketing mistake before revealing the expert verdict.

The simulator is designed for beginners while remaining engaging for marketers, students, and business professionals.

---

##  Features

-  Multiple color themes (including Claude Orange)
-  Detective-inspired premium dark UI
-  Randomized fictional marketing case files
-  Interactive investigation board
-  Draggable evidence cards
-  Marketing mistake identification challenge
-  Campaign performance analysis
-  Interactive metrics and visualizations
-  Expert explanation and learning report
-  Replay with new randomized cases
-  Fully responsive design
-  Runs completely offline

---


##  Gameplay Flow

1. Choose a preferred color theme
2. Accept a marketing investigation
3. Review the case file
4. Analyze campaign evidence
5. Drag and organize clues
6. Identify the primary marketing mistake
7. Submit your investigation
8. Review the expert verdict
9. Explore the Marketing Learning Report
10. Replay with a new randomized case

---

##  Learning Outcomes

- Understand common marketing mistakes
- Interpret campaign performance metrics
- Analyze customer feedback
- Connect marketing data with business outcomes
- Improve strategic thinking
- Learn marketing through interactive problem-solving

---


##  Key Learnings

- Successful marketing depends on data-driven decisions.
- Campaign metrics tell a story when analyzed together.
- Root-cause analysis is more valuable than surface-level observations.
- Customer feedback often reveals hidden marketing problems.
- Interactive simulations make complex concepts easier to understand.
