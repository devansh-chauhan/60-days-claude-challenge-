# Day 23 — Customer & MVP Blueprint

## Overview

Today’s task focused on transforming a startup idea into a customer-focused product strategy. Using the Startup Validation Report from Day 22 as input, I generated a complete Customer & MVP Blueprint to better understand target users, pain points, product requirements, pricing, risks, and execution priorities.

## Objective

Build a structured blueprint that answers:

* Who are the ideal customers?
* What problems are they facing?
* What features should be built first?
* What should be avoided during the MVP stage?
* How should the product be positioned and priced?
* What actions should the founder take in the next 30 days?

## Startup Concept

**AI-Powered Placement & Interview Preparation Platform**

A platform designed to help engineering students improve placement readiness through:

* ATS Resume Analysis
* AI Mock Interviews
* Skill Gap Assessment
* Placement Readiness Tracking
* Personalized Learning Roadmaps

## Key Deliverables

### Customer Research

* Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
* Buyer Persona
* Top 10 Customer Pain Points
* Customer Journey Mapping
* Buying Triggers & Objections

### Product Strategy

* MVP Recommendation
* Success Metrics
* MoSCoW Prioritization
* Pricing Hypothesis
* Risk Assessment

### Founder Execution Plan

* 30-Day MVP Roadmap
* Top 10 Founder Actions
* Product-Market Fit Indicators
* Validation Priorities

## Key Learnings

* Students need personalized guidance more than additional learning content.
* Resume optimization and interview preparation are the strongest MVP entry points.
* Validation should begin with student interviews before building complex features.
* A focused MVP solves a single critical problem better than a feature-heavy platform.
* Early traction metrics matter more than extensive feature development.

## Outcome

The blueprint produced a strong demand signal with:

* Customer Clarity: 85/100
* Problem Severity: 88/100
* PMF Potential: 80/100
* MVP Readiness: 75/100
